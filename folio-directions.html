The next decision

Should the folio be drawn or built in space?

Both can feel illustrated. The difference is where the depth comes from: authored composition and layered drawings, or a real camera moving through ink-rendered geometry.

My bias before seeing the optionsKeep the home in 2D. Reserve spatial depth for the clock’s internal mechanism. That protects the warmth while preserving explanatory power.

Visual family A

Keep the folio 2D

Illustration owns the composition. Motion is selective: hands turn, pendulums swing, diagrams unfold and depth comes from layered parallax.

Warmest · most authored

Visual family B

Make it 3D isometric

A real camera and real depth remain, but the rendering is graphic: flat color, ink outlines, limited materials and authored compositions.

Most spatial · highest execution risk
3D · EXAMPLE 04

The cozy room diorama

A fixed isometric room gives the clock a home. Furniture and architecture are simplified, but every edge is inked and every surface uses illustration-like color rather than PBR plastic.

03 · The pendulumLength chooses the tempo.

Isometric room · toon materials · ink edges
Best atA sense of place

We can orbit, reveal the clock’s side and move naturally into cutaways.

MotionCamera and object choreography

Room walls open; the clock slides forward; the case becomes transparent.

RiskClosest to the current problem

If geometry or lighting slips, it becomes a prettier toy room.

3D · EXAMPLE 05

The ink-rendered cutaway

Skip the furnished room after the opening. Put the clock on an illustrated drafting stage where its mechanism can expand, rotate and annotate in real space.

3D · EXAMPLE 06

The isometric story tiles

Each major idea lives on its own little illustrated stage. Scroll replaces one tile with the next until they join into the full clock.

Story progressionRepeat. Swing. Count.

Isometric chapters · modular vignettes
Best atPlayful modular teaching

Each physics idea is approachable and visually self-contained.

MotionTile-to-tile transitions

Pieces migrate between stages and eventually snap together.

RiskLeast grown-up option

The modular stages can read like a board game if over-stylized.
